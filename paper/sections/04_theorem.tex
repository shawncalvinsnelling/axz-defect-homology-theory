\section{Main Theorem}
\begin{theorem}[Snelling Defect Homology Theorem]
A finite AXZ audit complex is closed if and only if $\dim H_R^{AXZ}=0$ and $\dim H_G^{AXZ}=0$. If either dimension is positive, the nonzero quotient classes are finite obstruction classes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
This is the standard finite-dimensional quotient-space criterion. The group $H_R^{AXZ}$ measures residual classes killed by the gate map but not generated by claims. The group $H_G^{AXZ}$ measures gate classes killed by the receipt map but not generated by residuals. Vanishing of both quotients is exactly the absence of surviving finite obstruction classes.
\end{proof}
