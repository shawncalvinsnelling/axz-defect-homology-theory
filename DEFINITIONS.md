# Definitions

An AXZ verification complex is a finite tuple:

\[
K=(C,R,G,H,\partial_1,\partial_2,\partial_3,\Gamma,\varepsilon,\rho,\tau)
\]

over \(\mathbb F_2\), where \(C\) is claim space, \(R\) residual/defect space, \(G\) gate space, and \(H\) receipt space.

Full chain condition:

\[
\partial_2\partial_1=0,\qquad \partial_3\partial_2=0.
\]

Residual defect homology:

\[
H_R^{AXZ}=\ker(\partial_2)/\operatorname{im}(\partial_1).
\]

Gate-receipt homology:

\[
H_G^{AXZ}=\ker(\partial_3)/\operatorname{im}(\partial_2).
\]

Nano-gap stability:

\[
\varepsilon_i < \Gamma_i.
\]
